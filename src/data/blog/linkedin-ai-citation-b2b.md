## Let me ask you something

When your ideal buyer asks ChatGPT about tools in your category…

**Is your brand even part of the answer?**

Not your website.  
Not your landing page.

Your name.

Because AI isn’t just indexing content anymore.  
It’s quoting people.

And more often than not, those people are on LinkedIn.

---

## The way B2B SEO used to work

You:
- Write blog posts  
- Optimize for keywords  
- Rank on Google  
- Capture demand  

That model still works.

But it’s no longer complete.

Because buyers are now starting with:
- ChatGPT  
- Perplexity  
- Google AI Mode  

These platforms don’t rank content.  
They generate answers.

---

## The funnel didn’t disappear — it moved inside AI

Earlier:

Google → Blog → Comparison → Website → Demo  

Now, most of this happens inside AI.

### Awareness  
“What tools are used for X?”  
If you’re not mentioned, you don’t exist.

### Consideration  
“Best tools for X”  
AI defines your positioning.

### Decision  
“Which tool should I choose?”  
Only then does the buyer click.

---

## Where AI gets its answers

Research (Semrush, 2026):

- LinkedIn appears in ~11% of AI responses  
- #2 most cited domain after Reddit  
- ChatGPT cites LinkedIn in 14.3%  

Profound (2026):

- LinkedIn jumped from #11 → #5  
- #1 for professional queries  

---

## People > company pages

- 59% of citations come from individuals  
- Personal posts get 561% more reach  

AI trusts people, not logos.

---

## What gets cited

Most cited posts:
- 15–25 reactions  
- 5+ posts/month  

What works:
- Educational content  
- 500–2000 words  
- Original insights  

---

## The ghost citation problem

AI can use your content without crediting you.

- Attribution drops from 53% → 10.6% without branding  

---

## SEO vs GEO

SEO:
- Rank  
- Get clicks  

GEO:
- Get cited  
- Shape perception  

---

## Final thought

The question is:

**Are you showing up in AI answers at all?**
